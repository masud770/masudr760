Academic Journey

I completed my B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering from Daffodil International University (DIU) with a CGPA of 3.52, where I built a strong foundation in programming, algorithms, and system design.

Alongside academic coursework, I gained practical experience in computer networking, software development, and software testing, which strengthened my ability to design, implement, and validate real-world systems.

My undergraduate thesis, “Reasoning Over Context in Bangla: A Generative QA Approach to Factoid Understanding Using LLM”, focused on developing a Bangla question-answering system using transformer-based models. This work also involved building and curating a large-scale Bangla QA dataset, contributing to low-resource NLP research.

In addition, I served as Vice President of the DIU NLP Lab, where I contributed to research collaboration, AI-focused projects, and knowledge-sharing initiatives within the lab community.

My academic journey reflects a consistent focus on Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Natural Language Processing, combined with hands-on engineering experience and research-driven problem solving.
